Personality Disorder

Overview:
Personality disorders are a group of conditions characterized by long-term patterns of thinking and behaving that differ markedly from cultural expectations and cause distress or impairment.

Common features:
- Rigid, unhealthy patterns of thinking and relating to others
- Emotional instability, difficulties in relationships

Support & treatment:
Therapies such as dialectical behavior therapy (DBT) and schema therapy, alongside support networks, can help manage symptoms and improve functioning.
